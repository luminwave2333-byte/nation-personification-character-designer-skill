## China / textiles / calligraphy

7. The Metropolitan Museum of Art, Chinese Textiles: An Introduction to the Study of their History, Sources, Technique, Symbolism, and Use
   https://www.metmuseum.org/met-publications/chinese-textiles-an-introduction

8. The Metropolitan Museum of Art, China: Through the Looking Glass — Exhibition Galleries
   https://www.metmuseum.org/exhibitions/listings/2015/china-through-the-looking-glass/exhibition-galleries

Met notes that dragons in court-fashion imagery can invoke imperial authority, and that calligraphy has been treated by fashion designers as graphic textile pattern as well as language. The Skill uses this only as evidence for the general design mechanism “symbol → textile/graphic language.”

## Method note

The country seed library is a design inference layer, not an ethnographic encyclopedia. Any new nation or ethnic-group request should create a fresh evidence ledger from authoritative sources before asserting specific historical or cultural facts.
